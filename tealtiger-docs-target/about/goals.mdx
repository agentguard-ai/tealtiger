## Purpose

This document defines the **primary goals of TealTiger**.

These goals describe:
- What TealTiger is designed to achieve
- The problems it intentionally focuses on
- The outcomes users should expect when adopting it correctly

This document sets **direction**, not implementation detail.

---

## Core Goals of TealTiger

### 1. Provide Deterministic Governance for AI Workloads

TealTiger’s primary goal is to enforce **deterministic, policy‑driven governance** for AI systems.

For any decision, TealTiger should make it possible to answer:
- What policy was evaluated?
- What conditions were met?
- What action was taken?
- Why was this decision made?

Governance decisions must be:
- Predictable
- Reproducible
- Explainable

---

### 2. Enable Policy‑Based Control Across Cost, Security, and Reliability

TealTiger aims to provide a **single policy framework** that governs:
- AI cost usage
- Security exposure and risk
- Reliability and execution safety

Rather than siloed tools, TealTiger enables:
- Shared policy language
- Shared audit model
- Shared enforcement semantics

This creates consistent governance across AI systems.

---

### 3. Be SDK‑First and Developer‑Native

TealTiger is designed to be adopted **by developers**, not bolted on later.

Key goals:
- Minimal integration friction
- Clear APIs and SDKs
- Infrastructure‑friendly design
- No dependency on UI‑first workflows

Governance should fit naturally into application code and pipelines.

---

### 4. Make Governance Decisions Explicit and Auditable

Every decision made by TealTiger should produce:
- A clear outcome (allow, deny, modify, redact)
- Structured audit events
- Stable reason codes
- Consistent telemetry

Auditability is not an afterthought — it is a first‑class output.

---

### 5. Preserve Separation of Concerns

TealTiger aims to **govern AI behavior without owning adjacent responsibilities**.

It intentionally separates itself from:
- Model hosting
- Identity management
- Secrets handling
- Observability platforms
- FinOps systems

This keeps TealTiger focused, composable, and safe to adopt.

---

### 6. Support Scalable and Composable Policy Design

TealTiger’s policy system is designed to:
- Scale from simple to complex use cases
- Compose policies by concern
- Avoid monolithic rule sets

This allows teams to evolve governance safely as systems grow.

---

### 7. Reduce Surprise and Increase Trust

A core goal of TealTiger is to **reduce surprise**.

Users should never experience:
- Random enforcement
- Undocumented behavior
- Hidden heuristics
- Silent policy changes

Trust is built through consistency and transparency.

---

### 8. Enable Governance Without Slowing Innovation

TealTiger aims to act as a **guardrail, not a blocker**.

Well‑designed governance should:
- Catch risky behavior early
- Fail fast when constraints are violated
- Allow safe experimentation within boundaries

Governance should enable teams to move faster — not slower.

---

### 9. Provide a Stable Foundation for Future Expansion

In v1.1.0, TealTiger focuses on **core governance primitives**.

These primitives are designed to support:
- Expanded controls in v1.2.x
- Platform‑level coordination in v1.3.x
- Broader AI governance use cases over time

Stability today enables safe evolution tomorrow.

---

## What Success Looks Like

TealTiger is successful when:

- Developers understand why decisions happen
- Security teams can trust audit evidence
- Cost controls are predictable and enforceable
- Governance feels boring, reliable, and explicit

If governance feels magical or surprising, the goal has not been met.
